Exercises for weight gain



1. Pullups

Step 1: Grip the pullup bar with both hands. Your palms should face away from you. Keep your arms shoulder-width apart.
Step 2: Pull yourself up enough to hang off the bar so that your feet aren’t touching the ground and your arms are straight.
Step 3: Continue to pull yourself up until your chin is above the bar.
Step 4: Slowly lower yourself down so that your arms are straight again.
Step 5: Repeat as many times as you want.

2. Squats

Step 1: Stand up straight so that your feet are hips-width distance apart.
Step 2: Put your hands on your hips and flex your abdominal muscles.
Step 3: Begin lowering yourself down using only your legs, as if you’re about to sit down, and get into a seated position until your thighs are parallel to the ground. Keep your upper body as still as possible.
Step 4: Raise yourself back up to your original position.
Step 5: Repeat as many times as you want or you can.

3. Lunges

Step 1: Stand up straight, flexing your abdominal muscles.
Step 2: Extend one leg like you’re taking a step, then lean forward like you’re kneeling until your knees are at 90-degree angles.
Step 3: Push back on your heel to lift yourself back up to your initial position.
Step 4: Repeat as many times as you feel comfortable on one leg.
Step 5: Repeat for the other leg.

4. Bench Press

Step 1: Lie on your back on the bench. If the bench has a rack for the bar, face the bar. If there’s no rack, hold the bar carefully and slowly lie backward on the bench until you’re comfortable.
Step 2: If there’s a rack, grab the bar with both hands, including your thumbs. Feel free to spread your fingers a bit.
Step 3: Extend your arms to take the bar out of the rack.
Step 4: Slowly lower your arms to bring the bar down to your chest.
Step 5: Slowly straighten your arms and raise the bar back toward the rack. If there’s no rack, make sure you have the strength to sit back up after you’re done.
Step 6: Repeat steps 4 and 5 as many times as you feel comfortable.

5. Push-Ups

Step 1: Get down on all fours, placing your hands slightly wider than your shoulders.
Step 2: Straighten your arms and legs.
Step 3: Lower your body until your chest nearly touches the floor.
Step 4: Pause, then push yourself back up.

6. Overhead press

Step 1: Grip the bar with your hands about shoulder-width apart.
Step 2: Lift the bar up to just above the front of your chest, even with your shoulders.
Step 3: Slowly lift the bar above you until your arms are straight. Keep your elbows locked and raise your shoulders like you’re shrugging.
Step 4: Slowly lower the bar back to shoulder height.
Step 5: Repeat steps 3 and 4 as many times as comfortable.
