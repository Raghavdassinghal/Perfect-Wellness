Exercises for weight loss




1. Planks

Step 1: Position your elbows directly beneath your shoulders.
Step 2: Make and maintain a straight body line from head to heels.
Step 3: Squeeze your butt and the fronts of your thighs.
Step 4: Brace your abs like you're going to take a punch.
Step 5: Advance the exercise.

2. Bicycle Crunch

Step 1: Lie flat on the floor with your lower back pressed to the ground (pull your navel in to also target your deep abs).
Step 2: Put your hands behind your head, then bring your knees in towards your chest and lift your shoulder blades off the ground, but be sure not to pull on your neck.
Step 3: Straighten your right leg out to about a 45-degree angle to the ground while turning your upper body to the left, bringing your right elbow towards the left knee. Make sure your rib cage is moving and not just your elbows.
Step 4: Now switch sides and do the same motion on the other side to complete one rep.
Step 5: Do three sets of 20 reps.

3. Jogging

Step 1: Avoid hitting the ground heel first. This is bad for your knees. Instead, try to hit the ground with the flat of your foot or, ideally, on the front/balls of your feet.
Step 2: Bend your arms at a 90Â° angle.
Step 3: Keep your head straight. Don't look down any more than you have to.

4. Lunge Twist

Step 1: Stand straight with your feet hip-width apart and your arms lifted at the front.
Step 2: Take a step forward with your right leg, bend both knees and rotate your torso to the right.
Step 3: Return to the starting position and repeat the movement on the left side.
Step 4: Alternate sides until set is complete.

5. Push-Ups

Step 1: Get down on all fours, placing your hands slightly wider than your shoulders.
Step 2: Straighten your arms and legs.
Step 3: Lower your body until your chest nearly touches the floor.
Step 4: Pause, then push yourself back up.

6. Reverse Crunch

Step 1: To perform a reverse crunch, lie flat on your back with your hands beneath your hips.
Step 2: Bend your knees and lift them towards your head, drawing them upward slightly at the end of the movement.
Step 3: Lower your feet back down just above the floor to complete one repetition.
